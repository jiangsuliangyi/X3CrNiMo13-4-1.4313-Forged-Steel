Technical Overview: X3CrNiMo13-4 (1.4313) Martensitic Stainless Steel Forgings

X3CrNiMo13-4 (EN numerical designation 1.4313, commonly referred to as F6NM) is a high-strength martensitic stainless steel with a specialized chemical composition. By adding Nickel and Molybdenum to the Chromium-based structure, this grade offers significantly better toughness, weldability, and corrosion resistance than standard 13% Chromium martensitic steels.

1. Chemical Composition

According to EN 10088-3 standards, the precise balance of alloying elements ensures the material's superior performance in aggressive environments:

Element Content (%) Element Content (%)
Carbon (C) ≤ 0.05 Nickel (Ni) 3.50 - 4.50

Manganese (Mn) ≤ 1.50 Silicon (Si) ≤ 0.70

2. Mechanical Properties (Quenched & Tempered)

The 1.4313 forgings are known for their exceptional mechanical strength and low-temperature impact toughness. Typical properties for common heat treatment conditions (QT780 and QT900) include:

QT780 780 - 980 ≥ 620 ≥ 15 ≥ 70
QT900 900 - 1100 ≥ 800 ≥ 12 ≥ 50

3. Key Applications

Due to its excellent resistance to cavitation and erosion-corrosion, X3CrNiMo13-4 is a preferred choice for high-stress components in various industries:

  • Hydropower: Pelton and Francis turbine runners, blades, and guide vanes.
  • Oil & Gas: Compressor components, valve bodies, and high-pressure pump shafts.
  • Chemical Industry: Centrifuge parts and offshore engineering components.
  • Aerospace: High-strength structural parts requiring moderate corrosion resistance.

4. Forging and Heat Treatment

The forging process for 1.4313 requires strict temperature control, typically between 1150°C and 900°C. To achieve the desired properties, a specialized double-tempering process is often employed. This ensures the transformation of retained austenite into stable tempered martensite, maximizing the material's ductility and impact resistance even at sub-zero temperatures.
